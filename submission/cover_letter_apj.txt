To the Editors, The Astrophysical Journal

Dear Editors,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "Time-Domain Imaging of
Rotating, Cloud-Covered Exoplanets with the Solar Gravitational Lens:
Reconstruction Algorithms, Observing-Cadence Requirements, and Mission
Targets," for publication in ApJ.

The solar gravitational lens (SGL) is the only known route to
multipixel imaging of terrestrial exoplanets, and its literature has
recently converged on a single dominant open problem: the target
rotates and its clouds evolve while the image plane is raster-scanned,
so the data are temporally aliased. Toth (2025) concluded from
clouds-as-noise averaging that Earth-like cloud cover may defeat the
concept; Turyshev (2026) explicitly defers a dynamic retrieval pipeline
as a next-stage deliverable. This manuscript supplies that missing
analysis. Its contributions are:

1. A validated, open, end-to-end simulator reproducing the published
   SGL kernel, photon-rate, and deconvolution-penalty benchmarks.
2. A time-domain inversion estimator that removes the rotation problem
   outright (r = 0.99 vs the previously reported SNR <~ 2).
3. The first quantitative cloud-degradation curves for SGL imaging,
   showing clouds exceed coronal noise by an order of magnitude and
   identifying the covariance structure responsible.
4. A new observing-cadence law - at fixed photons and duration, many
   short revisits beat few long dwells by factors of ~4 - which
   converts cloud cover from a feasibility veto into a swarm-sizing
   requirement, and a hard precursor requirement on the rotation
   period (1e-4).
5. A ranked, quantitative target study of the five nearest confirmed
   potentially habitable planets, including focal-line placements,
   image-plane dynamics, and transportation/power budgets tied to the
   current architecture literature.

All results are reproducible from the included code (fixed seeds; ~40
core-minutes). I suggest as potential referees researchers active in
SGL imaging theory, exocartography, and deep-space mission design. This
work has not been submitted elsewhere. I have no conflicts of interest
and received no external funding.
